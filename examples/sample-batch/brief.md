# Task Brief — Opinion Article on School Uniforms

**Genre:** Opinion article
**Length:** 200–300 words
**Time allotted:** 1 lesson (~50 minutes)
**Expected level:** Lower-intermediate (roughly CEFR B1)
**Rubric:** `rubrics/english-cefr.md`

## Prompt

> "Schools should require students to wear uniforms." Do you agree or disagree? Write a short opinion article explaining your view.

## Required structural elements

Each piece must include all five of the following. Missing any one triggers the rubric's grade-ceiling (max overall = Developing).

1. **Introduction** — a hook (not just a restatement of the prompt) and a clear statement of the student's position.
2. **Reason 1** — a first reason for the position, supported by an example or explanation.
3. **Reason 2** — a second, *distinct* reason for the position, supported by an example or explanation.
4. **Counterargument and response** — what someone who disagreed might say, and a direct response.
5. **Conclusion** — restates the position and offers a final thought (not just a one-line summary).

## Bonus features (not required; credit when present and used purposefully)

- Rhetorical question used at an argumentative pivot (not just decoration).
- Varied sentence openers: *Although, However, Despite this, In contrast, Therefore.*
- A named, specific example (a real school, a specific country, a specific statistic).
