Walk into any school in Japan and you will see something interesting: every student is wearing almost exactly the same outfit. Is this a good idea? I strongly believe that schools should require uniforms, and there are two main reasons why.

First, uniforms create a sense of belonging. When you walk into a classroom and everyone is wearing the same blazer, you immediately feel like part of a group. This matters more than people think. Research from a UK study in 2018 showed that schools with strict uniform policies reported lower rates of bullying related to appearance. When all students look similar, the social pressure to wear expensive brands disappears, and students from different family backgrounds are on equal ground.

Second, uniforms help students switch into "school mode." Just as a doctor puts on a white coat to start work, a student putting on a uniform signals to themselves that the school day has begun. This small ritual can improve focus and discipline, especially for younger students who struggle with transitions.

Of course, some argue that uniforms suppress individuality and creativity. This is a fair point, but I would respond that real individuality does not come from clothing — it comes from ideas, talents, and how we treat others. A uniform does not erase who you are; it simply removes one distraction from the school environment.

In conclusion, uniforms are not just about clothes. They are about belonging, focus, and equality. Yes, the design could be more comfortable, but the principle is worth keeping. Schools should require uniforms — and design them to be ones students actually want to wear.
