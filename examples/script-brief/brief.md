# Task Brief — Short Script Writing

**Genre:** Short film / television script
**Length:** 1–3 handwritten pages, or ~250–500 words typed
**Expected level:** Lower-intermediate (CEFR B1 / IB MYP year 2)
**Rubric:** `rubrics/english-cefr.md`

## Prompt

Choose one situation. Write a short script (one to two scenes) showing how the characters respond.

- **Prompt A — Lunch Break Disagreement.** Two friends cannot agree on where to eat lunch. One wants something new; one wants their usual place.
- **Prompt B — Forgotten Homework.** A student arrives at school and realises they have left their homework at home. They have to decide what to do.
- **Prompt C — Robot at School.** A new student arrives in class. The other students slowly realise that the new student is not human.
- **Prompt D — Stuck in a Lift.** Two strangers are stuck in a lift between floors. One of them has a secret.

## Required structural elements

Each script must include all four of the following. Missing any one triggers the rubric's grade ceiling.

1. **Slugline** — a scene heading in the form `INT./EXT. LOCATION – TIME` (e.g. `INT. CLASSROOM – MORNING`).
2. **Action line** — at least one unlabelled descriptive line establishing what is happening in the scene (not a labelled "DESCRIPTION:" block).
3. **Dialogue** — at least one line of dialogue attached to a character name (e.g. `MARIA: I don't want to go there again.`).
4. **Parenthetical** — at least one short stage direction or emotion cue attached to a character's dialogue line, in brackets (e.g. `MARIA (nervous): Are you sure about this?`).

## Bonus features (not required; credit when present and used purposefully)

- Shot direction: `CUT TO:`, `CLOSE-UP ON:`, `WIDE SHOT:`, `FADE OUT:`.
- Multiple scenes with distinct sluglines.
- A short twist or reveal that recontextualises an earlier line.

## Notes for the marker

- Use the **English-CEFR rubric** (`rubrics/english-cefr.md`).
- A *labelled* descriptive block (e.g. `DESCRIPTION:` or `Direction:` or `words:`) is a common attempt at an action line but does not strictly satisfy the convention. Mark it as a partial credit — Text Type Conventions sits one band lower than it would if the action line were unlabelled.
- A character cue in brackets (e.g. `(MARIA)` used as a speaker label) is not a parenthetical. Parentheticals are attached to a *named* speaker and modify how a line of dialogue is delivered.
- Bonus features should never lift a band above 5 on their own, but they can be the deciding factor when synthesis hovers between 4 and 5.
